Before You Begin: The 15-Minute Prep

Before we dive in, let's get our ducks in a row. Taking just 15 minutes to gather this information now will make the next five steps incredibly fast and smooth. Think of it as organizing your toolbox before you start a big job.

You'll want to have this handy:

  • Your Business Info: Your official business name, address, and phone number.
  • Your Logo (Optional but Recommended): A digital file (like a PNG or JPG) of your company logo.
  • Your Business Hours: Your standard hours of operation, including any weekend or emergency hours.
  • Your List of Services: A simple list of the main services you offer (e.g., "Water Heater Repair," "Drain Cleaning," "Emergency Leak Fix").
  • Your Customer List (Optional): If you have an existing list of customers in a spreadsheet (like Excel or Google Sheets) or in QuickBooks, having it ready will make Step 1 even easier.

Got it? Great. Let's get started. By the end of this guide, you'll have a system that works for you, even when you're sleeping.

Step 1: Set Your Service Area & Business Profile

The Goal: To tell your Lead Bot who you are, when you work, and, most importantly, *where* you work. This ensures you only get leads for profitable jobs in your target area.

Why This Matters

How many times have you wasted 10 minutes on the phone with a potential customer only to find out they live 50 miles away? Setting your service area is the first and most critical step to getting qualified leads. It instantly filters out time-wasters and ensures that every lead your bot interacts with is a job you can actually do. This is the foundation of an efficient and profitable plumbing business. It's like building the frame of the house before you put up the walls.

How to Do It (The 10-Minute Walkthrough)

  1. Log In and Find "Settings": Open your Lead Bot dashboard. Look for a gear icon ⚙️ or a menu item labeled "Settings" or "Business Profile."
  2. Enter Your Business Info: You'll see fields for your company name, phone number, and address. Fill these out carefully. This information will be used in automated texts and emails to your customers, so professionalism counts! Upload your logo if you have it ready.
  3. Define Your Hours: Find the "Business Hours" or "Operating Hours" section. Set your standard Monday-to-Friday hours. Most tools will also have an option for "Emergency Hours" or "24/7 Service." Be sure to set this correctly, as it will tell your bot when to try and book an appointment versus when to simply take a message and promise a callback.
  4. Draw Your Service Area on the Map: This is the fun part! Find the "Service Area" section. You'll see an interactive map. Most tools allow you to either draw a custom shape (like a polygon) around your target neighborhoods or simply enter a list of ZIP codes you service. Be realistic. It's better to start with a smaller, denser service area and expand later.
💡 Pro Tip: Create multiple, color-coded service zones. You could have a "Primary Zone" (your most profitable, easy-to-reach areas) and a "Secondary Zone" (areas you'll go to but might charge a small travel fee for). Your bot can be configured to mention this fee automatically during the booking process!

Step 2: Activate Your 24/7 AI Receptionist

The Goal: To connect your main business phone number to the Lead Bot so it can automatically answer calls you miss and convert those callers into booked jobs.

Why This Matters

A missed call is a missed job. It's that simple. When a homeowner has a burst pipe, they don't leave a message and wait patiently. They hang up and call the next plumber on the list. This step turns your voicemail from a lead graveyard into your single best lead-capturing tool. By activating the AI Receptionist, you are essentially hiring a perfectly trained, always-on employee who never calls in sick, never takes a vacation, and is 100% focused on booking you high-paying jobs.

How to Do It (The 15-Minute Setup)

  1. Find Your Lead Bot Number: In your dashboard, look for a section called "My Number," "Call Forwarding," or "AI Receptionist." The system will have assigned you a unique, new phone number. Don't worry, you won't have to give this number out to customers.
  2. Log In to Your Phone Provider: Open a new tab and log in to your account with your business phone provider (e.g., Verizon, AT&T, Vonage, Google Voice, etc.). If your business number is just your personal cell, you can do this directly from your phone's settings.
  3. Set Up Conditional Call Forwarding: You're looking for a setting called "Conditional Call Forwarding" or sometimes "No Answer/Busy Call Forwarding." This is the key. You are NOT forwarding all your calls. You are only telling your phone provider to forward calls under specific conditions:
    • When your line is **busy** (you're on another call).
    • When you **don't answer** the call after a certain number of rings (e.g., 4 rings).
    This means you still have the chance to answer the phone yourself! The bot only jumps in to save the day when you can't.
  4. Enter Your Lead Bot Number: In the forwarding settings, paste or type in the unique Lead Bot number from Step 1. Save your changes.

That's it! Your safety net is now active.

Step 3: Customize Your Automated Customer Texts

The Goal: To review and lightly edit the pre-built SMS/text message templates for things like appointment confirmations and reminders, so they sound authentic to your business.

Why This Matters

Automation doesn't have to sound robotic. A simple, professional text message confirming an appointment can dramatically reduce no-shows. An automated "Your technician is on the way!" text provides a world-class customer experience that sets you apart from less organized competitors. And a friendly follow-up text asking for a review is the single easiest way to build your 5-star reputation on sites like Google and Yelp. This step turns your bot from a simple booking tool into a powerful customer communication platform. It builds trust and makes your business look incredibly professional.

How to Do It (The 10-Minute Review)

  1. Find "Automations" or "Templates": In your dashboard, navigate to the section for automated communications. This might be called "Automations," "Workflows," or "Customer Notifications."
  2. Review the Key Templates: You don't need to write anything from scratch! The system will already have proven, pre-built templates for the most important events. The top three to review are:
    • Appointment Confirmation: Sent immediately after a job is booked.
    • Appointment Reminder: Sent 24 hours before the appointment.
    • Review Request: Sent an hour after the job is marked as complete.
  3. Add Your Personal Touch: Read through the templates. The content will be great, but you might want to make small tweaks to make it sound more like you. For example, you could change "Your technician" to "Your expert plumber" or add your company slogan to the end. Keep it short, friendly, and professional.
💡 Pro Tip: Look for a feature called "Personalization Tokens" or "Merge Fields." This allows you to automatically insert the customer's name, appointment time, or your technician's name into the text. For example: "Hi [Customer Name], this is a friendly reminder of your appointment with [Technician Name] tomorrow at [Appointment Time]." This makes your automated messages feel personal and highly professional.

Step 4: Add the "Book Now" Button to Your Website

The Goal: To capture leads directly from your website visitors by giving them a simple, instant way to schedule a job online.

Why This Matters

More and more customers, especially younger ones, prefer not to pick up the phone. They want to book online, on their own time (even if it's 2 AM). If your website's only option is a "Contact Us" form that you'll answer tomorrow, you are losing those customers to competitors who offer instant online booking. Adding a "Book Now" widget is like opening a new, fully automated front door to your business. It's a powerful tool that transforms your website from a simple brochure into a lead-generating machine. For more on this, see our guide on the importance of a modern CRM for lead management.

How to Do It (The 5-Minute Copy & Paste)

  1. Find the "Widget" or "Website" Section: In your Lead Bot dashboard, look for a menu item for your website booking widget.
  2. Customize the Look (Optional): You'll likely have a few simple options to change the color of the button to match your website's branding.
  3. Copy the Code Snippet: You will see a small box of code. Don't be intimidated! You don't need to understand what it means. Just click the "Copy" button.
  4. Paste the Code on Your Website: Log in to the backend of your website (e.g., WordPress, Squarespace, Wix). If you have a web developer, you can simply email them this snippet of code. If you manage the site yourself, you're looking for an option to add "custom HTML" or "embed code." This is often in the header or footer section of your site's settings. Paste the code you copied and click "Save."

When you visit your website now, you should see a new "Book Now" or "Schedule Service" button. You've just opened for business, 24/7.

Step 5: Run Your First Test & Watch the Magic Happen

The Goal: To build confidence in your new system by running a quick test to see your new AI Receptionist in action.

Why This Matters

Trust is earned. Before you bet your business on this new tool, you need to see it work with your own eyes. A successful test will give you the peace of mind that your system is set up correctly and is ready to start capturing real leads and booking real jobs. It's the final quality check before you put your new machine to work.

How to Do It (The 5-Minute Test)

  1. The Missed Call Test: Grab your personal cell phone (or ask a friend or family member to help). Call your main business number. Let it ring until the call forwards. Don't answer.
  2. Interact with the Bot: A moment later, you should receive an automatic text message from your Lead Bot number that says something like, "Hi, this is Frank's Plumbing. Sorry we missed your call. How can we help?" Try texting back, "My sink is leaking." Watch as the bot understands and guides you through the booking process.
  3. Check Your Dashboard: After you finish the interaction, open your Lead Bot dashboard. You should see a new lead, complete with the conversation history. If you booked an appointment, it should appear on your calendar.

Congratulations! You did it. You are now officially ready to stop losing leads and start growing your business. 🎉
